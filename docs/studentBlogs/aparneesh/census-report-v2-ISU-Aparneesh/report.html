For instance, counties with a higher median age may have a larger retired population, which would naturally lower the median income.

Conclusion

Among the three factors we examined, education appears to be the most significant predictor of higher median income. In other words, counties in Missouri tend to have higher median salaries as the percentage of residents with a college degree or higher increases.

Limitations

  • Correlation doesnâ€™t imply Causation
  • Simplification of Race
  • Estimates (Margin of Error)
  • Spatial Limitation (only limited to Missouri)
  • Multi-factor problem
